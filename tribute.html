About Abay Kunanbayev

Abay Kunanbayev (1845–1904) was a Kazakh poet, composer, philosopher, and cultural reformer. He is regarded as the founder of modern Kazakh literature and a key figure in the cultural identity of Kazakhstan. Through his works, Abay promoted enlightenment, education, moral values, and a synthesis of Kazakh traditions with global culture.

1845 – 1904

  1. 1845: Born in the Chingiz Mountains, Semipalatinsk region, Kazakhstan.
  2. 1855: Received traditional Islamic and Russian education.
  3. 1870s: Began writing poetry reflecting Kazakh life and philosophy.
  4. 1889: Published his first collection of poems.
  5. 1890s: Translated works of Pushkin, Goethe, Byron, and other classics into Kazakh.
  6. 1904: Passed away in his native land.

Achivements

  • Founder of modern Kazakh literature
  • Philosophical and moral teachings compiled in "The Book of Words" (Kara Sozder)
  • Promotion of education and enlightenment
  • Musical legacy with over 60 songs
  • Translations of world literature into Kazakh
